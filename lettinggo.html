Letting go of the addiction to "not enough" and discovering the richness that's already here.

Chapter: The Permission to Just Be

Finding Enoughness in a World That Always Wants More

There's something I've noticed about guided meditations and why they work. You'll notice there are questions that are asked throughout. Here's the thing—you don't actually have to answer them. You can if you want to, but the questions are really just prompts. They're invitations to notice whatever comes up within you naturally.

So if I ask something like, "Could you welcome this feeling?" you might notice a feeling arise. You might notice an answer of "no" come up inside. That's not a mistake. That's actually really great and helpful.

"There are no wrong answers here. No right answers either. Instead, as you allow yourself to simply notice whatever comes up, it all takes care of itself."

The Gift of Resistance

Feeling, not fighting

Resistance passes because you let yourself feel it — not because you suppressed it. When you just see it, it untangles by itself.

The power of "no"

Often saying "no" is more releasing than saying "yes" when you think you should. Honesty with where you are is the door.

The doing trance

The mind is wired for constant doing. That's why sitting still feels so hard — but the discomfort is just information.

Stories from the mat

Real moments that illuminate the teaching.

The ten‑dollar note

Remember primary school? A ten‑dollar note meant you could buy anything at the canteen. It felt like abundance. Now ten dollars feels like nothing. The money didn’t change – we learned to see it as “not enough.” That innocent feeling of plenty is still within you. You can choose to see through that lens again.

— from the March 4 session
The physics exam

I studied for hours. Went into the exam and it looked like another language. I just laughed. The stress you feel is never just yours – it’s collective. Seeing that loosens its grip.

Not having a toothache

When you have a toothache, you realise how good it felt not to have one. Enoughness is everywhere – the breath, organs working, the absence of pain. We just stopped looking.

The addiction to not feeling enough

It sounds strange, but there’s an addiction to “not enough.” It’s familiar, it’s what everyone else seems to feel. When you see it clearly, the pressure lessens. Life circumstances can shift to reflect your inner ease.
