From Personal Crisis to Gospel Mastery

I was fixing computers when it hit me: we approach human transformation all wrong. 1. The Broken Computer and the Broken Heart

Yesterday, I fixed two computers. Found another place on the motherboard to plug things into. Problem solved. It got me thinking about how we approach human brokenness. We're like technicians trying to fix spiritual and emotional issues with behavioral patches‚Äîcognitive behavioral therapy here, a new habit there. But what if the issue isn't on the surface? 2. Boyd K. Packer's Startling Insight

Apostle Boyd K. Packer taught something revolutionary: "A study of the doctrines of Jesus Christ will change attitudes and behavior faster than a study of behavior will change behavior." Let that sink in. He claimed that diving into gospel doctrine creates more rapid, profound transformation than any psychological method. This isn't anti-therapy; it's about understanding hierarchy of change. Behavior modification works at the surface. Doctrine rewires at the identity level.

3. My Personal Laboratory of Pain

I tested this principle in the crucible of my own life. Years ago, my marriage ended. My wife decided to leave, to go out partying, to not remain with me. 4. The Two-Year Scripture Experiment

For two years during my separation and divorce, I committed to an experiment: three chapters of the Book of Mormon daily, sometimes reading twenty chapters in a single park session. I read through the Book of Mormon five times in those twenty-four months. This wasn't casual reading; this was immersion therapy for the soul.
